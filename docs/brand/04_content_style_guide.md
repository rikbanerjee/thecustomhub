# The Custom Hub: Content Style Guide

This guide provides rules for writing all customer-facing content, including product titles, descriptions, and marketing copy. Our goal is to maintain a consistent, elevated brand voice.

## 1. Product Naming

- **Formula:** `[Adjective] + [Product Type] with "[Design Name]"`
- **Examples:**
    - "Classic Crewneck with 'Mountain Sunrise'"
    - "Glossy Ceramic Mug with 'Floral Monogram'"
    - "Cozy Throw Pillow with 'Abstract Waves'"
- **Capitalization:** Use Title Case for product names.

## 2. Product Descriptions

- **Structure:**
    1.  **Opening Hook (1 sentence):** Start with an evocative sentence that captures the feeling of the design.
    2.  **Key Features (Bulleted List):** List 2-4 key features of the physical product. Focus on quality and material.
    3.  **The "Why" (1-2 sentences):** Connect the design to a feeling, a place, or an idea. Explain why this product is special.
    4.  **Call to Action (Optional):** A gentle nudge, e.g., "A perfect gift for the art lover in your life."

- **Example:**

    > **Product Name:** Artisan Canvas Tote with 'City Sketch'
    >
    > **Description:**
    >
    > Capture the energy of the city with this beautifully rendered urban sketch.
    >
    > - **Durable & Spacious:** Crafted from heavy-duty 12oz cotton canvas, ready for your daily adventures.
    > - **Vibrant, Lasting Print:** Our eco-friendly printing process ensures the design stays sharp and brilliant.
    > - **Comfortable to Carry:** Features reinforced, wide-set straps for all-day comfort.
    >
    > Whether you're running errands or heading to a cafe, this tote adds a touch of artistic flair to any outfit.
    >

## 3. General Tone Reminders

- **Do:** Use positive, inspiring language. Focus on benefits and feelings.
- **Don't:** Use generic phrases ("high quality," "great product"). Show, don't just tell.
- **Do:** Speak directly to the customer ("your new favorite...").
- **Don't:** Use jargon or overly technical terms.
